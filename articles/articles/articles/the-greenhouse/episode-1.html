Episode 1: The Walls Are the Engine

When people first hear about the greenhouse, they assume it’s about food. Food is what it produces. But food is not why it exists.

The walls of the greenhouse are the engine of the entire community. They rise forty feet high, layered with vertical growing systems, designed for reliability rather than novelty.

By carrying the burden of survival quietly in the background, the walls allow life in the foreground to soften.

The walls are not the heart of the greenhouse. They are the engine.
